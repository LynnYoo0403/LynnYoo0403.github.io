I am a computational design researcher investigating how ecological intelligence, environmental simulation, and digital fabrication can inform the development of adaptive architectural systems. I am currently a PhD candidate at the Southern University of Science and Technology (SUSTech), where my research focuses on computational approaches for sustainable architectural design, particularly ecological façade systems and environmentally responsive structures. My research aims to bridge the gap between digital design intelligence and environmental performance by developing data-driven approaches for the contemporary built environment. My work has been published in Automation in Construction and presented at international conferences in the CAAD community. I also serve as a reviewer for international conferences, including ACADIA, CAADRIA, CAADFutures, CDRF, and xArch.
